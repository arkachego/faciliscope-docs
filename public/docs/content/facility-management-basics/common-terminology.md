# Common Terminology

This glossary defines common terms used in Facility Management and FaciliScope.

## A

**Asset**
A physical item that requires maintenance, such as equipment, machinery, or infrastructure. Assets are tracked throughout their lifecycle.

**Asset Tag**
A unique identifier assigned to an asset for tracking purposes. Often displayed as a QR code for quick scanning.

**Assignment**
The act of assigning a resource (work order, asset, facility) to one or more users. FaciliScope supports multiple user assignments.

## B

**Budget**
A financial plan that allocates funds for facility operations, maintenance, and projects over a specific period.

**Budget Transaction**
A financial record of income or expense related to a budget, such as work order costs or purchase orders.

## C

**Corrective Maintenance**
Reactive maintenance performed after a failure or problem is identified. Also called "repair maintenance" or "breakdown maintenance."

**Compliance Record**
A record tracking regulatory requirements, certifications, inspections, or safety standards that must be maintained.

## F

**Facility**
A building, property, or physical location that is managed. Facilities contain assets and spaces.

**Facility Management (FM)**
The practice of coordinating the physical workplace with people and work processes to ensure functionality, comfort, safety, and efficiency.

## I

**Inventory**
Parts, materials, and supplies used for maintenance and repairs. Tracked by quantity, location, and cost.

**Inspection**
A systematic examination of assets, facilities, or systems to assess condition, identify issues, or verify compliance.

## M

**Maintenance Schedule**
A recurring plan for preventive maintenance activities. Automatically generates work orders based on frequency.

**Maintenance Execution**
The actual performance of scheduled maintenance tasks, including checklist completion and documentation.

## P

**Preventive Maintenance (PM)**
Scheduled, proactive maintenance performed regularly to prevent equipment failures and extend asset life.

**Predictive Maintenance**
Maintenance based on condition monitoring and data analysis to predict when maintenance is needed.

**Purchase Order (PO)**
A document authorizing the purchase of goods or services from a vendor. Tracks procurement and spending.

**Priority**
The urgency level of a work order: Critical, High, Medium, or Low. Determines response time and resource allocation.

## R

**Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**
A security model that restricts access based on user roles. Each role has specific permissions and capabilities.

## S

**Service Level Agreement (SLA)**
A contract defining expected service levels, response times, and resolution deadlines for work orders or services.

**Status**
The current state of a resource. Work orders have statuses like Open, Assigned, In Progress, Completed, Closed.

**Space**
A room, area, or zone within a facility. Used for space management and asset location tracking.

## T

**Time Entry**
A record of time spent on a work order. Used to calculate labor costs and track productivity.

**Template**
A reusable work order template that standardizes common maintenance tasks and reduces data entry.

## V

**Vendor**
A supplier, contractor, or service provider that provides goods or services to the organization.

## W

**Work Order**
A task, maintenance request, or repair job that needs to be completed. The central mechanism for managing maintenance work.

**Work Order Number**
A unique identifier automatically assigned to each work order for tracking and reference.
